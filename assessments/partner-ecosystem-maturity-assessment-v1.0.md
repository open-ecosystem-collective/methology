## How to use this document

This assessment is a **conversation guide**, not a form to fill in.

The questions are prompts. The scores reflect what you observe and hear –
not what the client reports about themselves. A client who says "yes we have
a partner strategy" and cannot describe it scores a 2, not a 5.

Use the warning signals and strength signals in each dimension to calibrate.
The scoring guide in [`scoring-guide.md`](scoring-guide.md) covers edge cases
and mixed profiles.

## Dimension 1 – Partner Awareness

> *Do they know the ecosystem? Do they understand the language of partner programmes?*

**Corresponding OEC level:** Discovery Specialist (Level 1)

### Questions

**Q1.1** – Can you name 5 software vendors whose stack you build on –
and the name of their partner programme?

| Score | What it sounds like |
|---|---|
| 1 | Can name vendors but has no idea partner programmes exist |
| 2 | Aware of some programmes but vague ("I think AWS has something") |
| 3 | Knows 2–3 programmes by name, limited detail |
| 4 | Knows 4–5 programmes by name, can describe what they offer |
| 5 | Knows programmes in detail, has evaluated which tier to target |

**Score: &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; / 5** &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; Notes:

---

**Q1.2** – What is MDF (Market Development Funds)?
Have you ever considered applying for it?

| Score | What it sounds like |
|---|---|
| 1 | Does not know what MDF is |
| 2 | Has heard the term, cannot explain it |
| 3 | Knows what MDF is, never applied |
| 4 | Knows what MDF is, has considered applying or started the process |
| 5 | Has applied for MDF and received it at least once |

**Score: &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; / 5** &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; Notes:

---

**Q1.3** – Do you have any formal partnerships with software vendors –
even inactive ones?

| Score | What it sounds like |
|---|---|
| 1 | No formal partnerships of any kind |
| 2 | One informal or accidental partnership ("they emailed us") |
| 3 | One or two partnerships, not actively managed |
| 4 | Two or three partnerships, at least one actively managed |
| 5 | Multiple partnerships, clear understanding of what each offers |

**Score: &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; / 5** &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; Notes:

---

**D1 Total: &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; / 15**

**Warning signals (score 1–2):** "MDF? What's that?" · No formal partnerships despite intensive tool use · Vendor known but business context of their programme unknown · Partnerships formed reactively ("the sales rep called us")

**Strength signals (score 4–5):** Can name partner programmes specifically · Knows the difference between ISV, service partner, and technology partner programmes · At least one active, deliberately chosen partnership · Has evaluated which tier to target

## Dimension 2 – MDF Readiness

> *Do they have the prerequisites to apply for Market Development Funds?*

**Corresponding OEC level:** Assessment Lead (Level 2)

### Questions

**Q2.1** – Do you have technical certifications with any software vendor?
(e.g. AWS Certified Partner, Kubernetes Certified Service Provider,
HashiCorp Partner, Datadog Partner)

| Score | What it sounds like |
|---|---|
| 1 | No certifications despite intensive tool use |
| 2 | Individuals have certifications but not the company |
| 3 | Company holds 1–2 certifications, not current |
| 4 | Company holds 2–3 current certifications |
| 5 | Multiple current certifications, actively maintained |

**Score: &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; / 5** &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; Notes:

---

**Q2.2** – Do you have case studies, reference customers, or success stories
that could be used in an MDF application?

| Score | What it sounds like |
|---|---|
| 1 | No case studies, clients won't speak publicly |
| 2 | Work has been done but nothing documented |
| 3 | One internal case study exists, not published |
| 4 | One or two published or publishable case studies |
| 5 | Multiple case studies, at least one public reference customer |

**Score: &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; / 5** &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; Notes:

---

**Q2.3** – Have you ever submitted an MDF application –
or do you know concretely how to do it?

| Score | What it sounds like |
|---|---|
| 1 | No knowledge of the process |
| 2 | Knows MDF exists, assumes it is complicated and for large companies |
| 3 | Has researched the process, not yet applied |
| 4 | Knows the specific contact at one or more vendors for MDF |
| 5 | Has applied and received MDF at least once, even a small amount |

**Score: &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; / 5** &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; Notes:

---

**D2 Total: &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; / 15**

**Warning signals (score 1–2):** No certifications despite intensive tool use · "We have no case studies" – work done, nothing documented · MDF process unknown; thinks it is for large companies · No reference customer willing to speak publicly

**Strength signals (score 4–5):** At least 2 current technical certifications · Has 1–3 anonymised or public case studies · Knows which vendor contact handles MDF · Has applied for MDF, even a small amount

## Dimension 3 – Co-Sell Maturity

> *Are they actively collaborating with vendors and complementary partners?*

**Corresponding OEC level:** Collaboration Architect (Level 3)

### Questions

**Q3.1** – In the last 12 months, have you won a deal together
with a software vendor or a complementary partner company?

| Score | What it sounds like |
|---|---|
| 1 | No co-sell activity, concept unfamiliar |
| 2 | Aware of co-sell, never tried it |
| 3 | One informal co-sell attempt, outcome unclear |
| 4 | One successful co-sell in last 12 months |
| 5 | Multiple co-sell wins, repeatable process in place |

**Score: &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; / 5** &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; Notes:

---

**Q3.2** – Are there companies you work with regularly –
formally or informally – on client projects?

| Score | What it sounds like |
|---|---|
| 1 | "We work alone" – silo mentality |
| 2 | Know complementary companies but no active contact |
| 3 | One or two companies in loose contact |
| 4 | Two or three companies with active collaboration on projects |
| 5 | A clear network of complementary partners, actively managed |

**Score: &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; / 5** &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; Notes:

---

**Q3.3** – Can you name deals you probably would have won
with partner support? What was missing?

| Score | What it sounds like |
|---|---|
| 1 | Cannot identify any – no awareness of the pattern |
| 2 | "Maybe" – very vague, cannot name specifics |
| 3 | Can name one deal, not sure what was missing |
| 4 | Can name 2–3 deals and articulate what was missing (badge, reference, etc.) |
| 5 | Has a clear analysis and has already started addressing the gaps |

**Score: &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; / 5** &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; Notes:

---

**D3 Total: &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; / 15**

**Warning signals (score 1–2):** "We don't really work with others" · Deals lost because of missing partner badge – and they didn't know · Complementary companies known but no active contact · Co-sell as a concept unfamiliar

**Strength signals (score 4–5):** At least 1 co-sell win in last 12 months · 2–3 complementary companies with active contact · Can name specific lost deals and the missing element · Has started addressing the identified gaps

## Dimension 4 – Growth Architecture

> *Do they have a scalable structure for partner growth?*

**Corresponding OEC level:** Growth Strategist (Level 4)

### Questions

**Q4.1** – Do you have a partner strategy for the next 12 months –
even informally?

| Score | What it sounds like |
|---|---|
| 1 | No strategy, "we take whoever comes" |
| 2 | CEO has a rough idea, not communicated to anyone |
| 3 | Discussed internally, not written down |
| 4 | Written strategy or clearly communicated priorities |
| 5 | Documented strategy with OKRs, owned by a named person |

**Score: &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; / 5** &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; Notes:

---

**Q4.2** – Do you have defined criteria for which partners you want
and which you don't?

| Score | What it sounds like |
|---|---|
| 1 | No criteria, accept any partnership |
| 2 | Implicit criteria, not articulated |
| 3 | Can describe criteria verbally, not documented |
| 4 | Documented criteria, used in at least one partner decision |
| 5 | Clear tier structure with documented entry criteria |

**Score: &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; / 5** &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; Notes:

---

**Q4.3** – Do you track partner-originated revenue, partner-sourced leads,
or co-sell pipeline separately from direct revenue?

| Score | What it sounds like |
|---|---|
| 1 | No tracking, "everything just comes in somehow" |
| 2 | Tracked loosely in someone's head |
| 3 | Approximate tracking in spreadsheet |
| 4 | Tracked in CRM or structured system |
| 5 | Full visibility, reported regularly, used in decisions |

**Score: &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; / 5** &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; Notes:

---

**D4 Total: &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; / 15**

**Warning signals (score 1–2):** "We take every partner that comes" – no selection, no strategy · Revenue not tracked by source · No OKRs or goals addressing partner growth · CEO does everything – no delegated partner responsibility

**Strength signals (score 4–5):** Written or clearly communicated partner strategy · Partner-originated revenue tracked · Documented criteria: "we work with X because..." · At least one person responsible for partner topics (even part-time)

## Dimension 5 – Executive Alignment

> *Does C-level understand and prioritise the strategic role of partners?*

**Corresponding OEC level:** Executive Advisor (Level 5)

### Questions

**Q5.1** – Is the CEO or CTO actively involved in partner decisions –
consciously, not just when asked?

| Score | What it sounds like |
|---|---|
| 1 | CEO does not know which partnerships are active |
| 2 | CEO is involved reactively when problems arise |
| 3 | CEO is aware of the topic, not consistently engaged |
| 4 | CEO is involved in key partner decisions and follows progress |
| 5 | CEO can explain the partner strategy in 2 minutes without notes |

**Score: &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; / 5** &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; Notes:

---

**Q5.2** – Is there a dedicated partner budget in the annual plan –
or are partner activities funded from the general marketing pot?

| Score | What it sounds like |
|---|---|
| 1 | No partner budget of any kind |
| 2 | Partner activities funded ad hoc when someone asks |
| 3 | Informal allocation, not in the official budget |
| 4 | Dedicated line item in annual budget |
| 5 | Dedicated budget with clear allocation by vendor/programme |

**Score: &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; / 5** &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; Notes:

---

**Q5.3** – Are partner goals reflected in OKRs, annual goals,
or board/investor communications?

| Score | What it sounds like |
|---|---|
| 1 | Partner topics do not appear in strategic documents |
| 2 | Mentioned occasionally but not as a measurable goal |
| 3 | One partner-related goal exists somewhere |
| 4 | Partner OKRs or goals in annual planning |
| 5 | Board/investors are updated on partner progress regularly |

**Score: &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; / 5** &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; Notes:

---

**D5 Total: &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; / 15**

**Warning signals (score 1–2):** CEO does not know which partnerships are active · "Partner budget" does not exist · Partner topics absent from strategic documents · Partners seen as "nice to have" not as a growth lever

**Strength signals (score 4–5):** CEO can explain the partner strategy · Dedicated partner budget with purpose · Partner OKRs in annual planning · Board/investors informed about partner progress
