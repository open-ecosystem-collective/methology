# Scoring Guide

**Open Ecosystem Collective – How to Interpret Assessment Results**

Scores tell you where a company is. Profiles tell you what they need.
This guide helps you move from numbers to recommendations.

## Score bands

| Score | Band | Recommended engagement | Delivered by |
|---|---|---|---|
| 0 – 15 | Starting point | Discovery Sprint | Level 1+ |
| 16 – 30 | Aware but blocked | Full Assessment | Level 2+ |
| 31 – 45 | Engaged but unstructured | Collaboration Plan | Level 3+ |
| 46 – 60 | Structured, ready to scale | Growth Plan | Level 4+ |
| 61 – 75 | Optimised | Executive Advisory | Level 5 |

---

## The score is a starting point – the profile is the story

A total score of 22 can mean two very different things:

**Profile A:** D1=10, D2=8, D3=2, D4=1, D5=1  
→ They know the ecosystem and have MDF prerequisites, but have never tried
to collaborate. The gap is behaviour, not knowledge. A Collaboration Plan
may be more useful than a Full Assessment they mostly already know.

**Profile B:** D1=4, D2=4, D3=5, D4=5, D5=4  
→ Strong execution capability but weak foundation knowledge and MDF readiness.
They are doing co-sell informally and have growth thinking but are missing
the basics. A Full Assessment will surface the specific gaps.

Always look at the dimension scores before deciding on the engagement.

## The 5 most common profiles

### Profile 1 – Technically strong, partner-blind
**Typical score:** 5–10  
**Pattern:** D1=2, D2=1, D3=1, D4=1, D5=1  
**What it means:** Brilliant product, no partner awareness whatsoever.
Engineers run everything. CEO may be an engineer too.  
**Frequency:** ~45% of 5–20 employee Cloud Native companies  
**Right engagement:** Discovery Sprint to build awareness first.
Do not start with the full assessment – they do not yet have the vocabulary.

---

### Profile 2 – Programme-aware, not active
**Typical score:** 15–25  
**Pattern:** D1=4, D2=2, D3=2, D4=2, D5=2  
**What it means:** They know what partner programmes are and have maybe
joined one or two, but have not activated them. MDF is theoretical.
Co-sell has never happened.  
**Frequency:** ~30%  
**Right engagement:** Full Assessment to surface specific opportunities.
The biggest quick win here is usually a single MDF application.

---

### Profile 3 – Active but unstructured
**Typical score:** 25–38  
**Pattern:** D1=4, D2=3, D3=5, D4=2, D5=2  
**What it means:** They are co-selling informally, have some partnerships,
but track nothing and have no strategy. Growing through luck more than design.  
**Frequency:** ~15%  
**Right engagement:** Collaboration Plan to structure what is already working.
Focus on tracking, criteria, and formalising the best existing relationships.

---

### Profile 4 – Structured, ready to scale
**Typical score:** 40–55  
**Pattern:** D1=4, D2=4, D3=4, D4=4, D5=3  
**What it means:** Good foundations, active partnerships, some tracking.
C-level is engaged but partner work is still not a strategic priority in
planning and budget.  
**Frequency:** ~8%  
**Right engagement:** Growth Plan to build the architecture for scale.
Key lever: getting partner goals into OKRs and a dedicated budget.

---

### Profile 5 – Optimised
**Typical score:** 60–75  
**Pattern:** D1=5, D2=5, D3=5, D4=5, D5=4+  
**What it means:** Partner ecosystem is already a real function.
What they need is not a plan – it is a thinking partner at C-level.  
**Frequency:** ~2%  
**Right engagement:** Executive Advisory. Monthly retainer.
Focus on strategy and market positioning, not building basics.

## When to override the score band

The score band is a recommendation, not a rule. Override it when:

**Score says Full Assessment, but go lower (Discovery Sprint) if:**
- The company cannot describe their tech stack without help
- The CEO is confused by the questions in D1
- There are fewer than 3 people in the company

**Score says Collaboration Plan, but go lower (Full Assessment) if:**
- D2 is below 6 (MDF readiness is too low to build on)
- The company has had a bad partnership experience and needs to rebuild trust in the concept first

**Score says Growth Plan, but start with Collaboration Plan if:**
- There is no named person responsible for partner topics
- C-level is not present at the assessment conversation – a growth plan without executive buy-in will not be implemented

## Scoring calibration notes

**On self-reporting bias:**  
Clients tend to score themselves 1–2 points higher than reality.
When a client says "yes we have a partner strategy" and cannot describe it,
score a 2, not a 4. You are scoring what exists and functions, not what
they intend to have.

**On recency:**  
Weight the last 12 months heavily. A company that had an active MDF
programme three years ago but has done nothing since scores a 2 on Q2.3,
not a 4.

**On company size:**  
For companies of 5–8 people, adjust expectations slightly downward.
A score of 25 from a 6-person company is significantly more impressive
than a score of 25 from a 20-person company.

**On the NiB flag:**  
During D3 and D4, watch for patterns that suggest neurodiversity is
affecting the company's ability to collaborate externally – not as
a weakness, but as an unrecognised dynamic. If you see this, flag
it in your recommendation and consider suggesting the NiB workshop
as a companion to the main engagement.

## MDF potential estimation

Use this as a rough guide when filling in the MDF potential field:

| Vendor | Typical range for small partners | Prerequisites |
|---|---|---|
| AWS | USD 5'000 – 20'000/year | Select tier or above, case study |
| Google Cloud | USD 5'000 – 15'000/year | Partner advantage status |
| HashiCorp / IBM | USD 3'000 – 10'000/year | Select or Premier tier |
| Datadog | USD 5'000 – 15'000/year | Partner status + certification |
| Grafana Labs | USD 2'000 – 8'000/year | Silver tier or above |
| SUSE / Rancher | USD 3'000 – 10'000/year | Partner status |
| Red Hat | USD 5'000 – 15'000/year | Ready or Certified tier |

For a company with 3–4 active vendor relationships and the right
prerequisites, a realistic first-year MDF total is USD 15'000–40'000.
Most companies with a score below 30 are not yet claiming any of this.
