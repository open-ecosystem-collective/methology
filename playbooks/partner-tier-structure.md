# Partner Tier Structure

**Open Ecosystem Collective – How to Design Your Own Partner Programme**

Once a company has active partnerships and wants to scale them,
they need structure. This playbook guides you through designing
a simple, functional partner tier system.

**For:** Companies with Assessment score 46+ (Growth Architecture D4 ≥ 10)  
**Delivered by:** Level 4+  
**License:** CC-BY-SA 4.0

---

## When you need this

You need a partner tier structure when:
- You have more than 3 active partner relationships and managing them
  ad hoc is becoming inconsistent
- Partners are asking what they get from the relationship
- You want to prioritise which partners get your time and resources
- You are building toward a partner-led growth motion

You do not need this yet when:
- You have fewer than 3 active partners
- You have not yet completed a co-sell deal
- Your CEO is still managing all partner relationships personally

Build the structure after the relationships exist. Not before.

---

## Part 1 – Define your partner types

Not all partners are the same. Start by mapping who you actually work with:

| Partner type | What they do | How they create value for you |
|---|---|---|
| **Technology partner** | Their product integrates with yours | Credibility, joint go-to-market, MDF |
| **Service partner** | Complementary services, same client base | Co-sell, referrals, expanded capability |
| **Referral partner** | Sends you leads without delivering services | Pipeline, introductions |
| **Community partner** | Amplifies your presence in the ecosystem | Visibility, access, credibility |

You may not have all four types. Start with what exists.

---

## Part 2 – Design the tier structure

Three tiers is enough for most small Cloud Native companies.
Do not create five tiers when you have eight partners.

### Recommended structure

**Tier 1 – Strategic Partners**  
Your most important 2–3 partnerships. Active co-sell, joint go-to-market,
regular executive contact, dedicated resources from both sides.

**Tier 2 – Active Partners**  
Partners you work with regularly but not on a strategic level.
Established process for referrals or co-sell, occasional joint activities.

**Tier 3 – Community Partners**  
Companies you are in contact with, may refer occasionally,
but are not yet working together on deals. Low maintenance.

---

## Part 3 – Define criteria for each tier

Criteria make the structure objective. Without them, tier assignment
becomes political and inconsistent.

Fill in the minimum requirements for each tier:

### Tier 1 – Strategic Partner

| Criterion | Minimum requirement |
|---|---|
| Co-sell deals completed (last 12 months) | ≥ [number] |
| Joint revenue generated | ≥ CHF / USD / EUR [amount] |
| Technical integration or certification | ○ Required ○ Preferred |
| Executive contact established | ○ Required |
| Joint marketing activity | ≥ [number] per year |
| Mutual referral agreement | ○ Required |

### Tier 2 – Active Partner

| Criterion | Minimum requirement |
|---|---|
| Referrals or leads exchanged (last 12 months) | ≥ [number] |
| Joint deal or co-sell attempt | ≥ [number] |
| Regular contact cadence | ○ Monthly ○ Quarterly |

### Tier 3 – Community Partner

| Criterion | Minimum requirement |
|---|---|
| Active contact (not dormant) | ○ At least once per quarter |
| Potential fit identified | ○ Required |

## Part 4 – Define what each tier receives

Partners need to know what they get. Otherwise the tier is meaningless.

| Benefit | Tier 1 | Tier 2 | Tier 3 |
|---|---|---|---|
| Listed on your website as partner | ○ Yes | ○ Yes | ○ Optional |
| Co-marketing activities | ○ Joint planning | ○ On request | ○ No |
| Lead sharing | ○ Systematic | ○ Ad hoc | ○ No |
| Technical integration support | ○ Dedicated | ○ Best effort | ○ No |
| Executive relationship | ○ Quarterly review | ○ Annually | ○ No |
| MDF applications | ○ Joint pursuit | ○ On request | ○ No |
| Case study development | ○ Priority | ○ On request | ○ No |
| Early access to new services/products | ○ Yes | ○ No | ○ No |

## Part 5 – Create a simple partner agreement

For Tier 1 and Tier 2, a brief written agreement prevents misunderstandings.
Keep it to one page.

**What to include:**

```
PARTNER AGREEMENT – [Your company] and [Partner company]

Tier: [1 / 2]
Date: [date]
Review date: [12 months from signing]

PURPOSE
[One sentence describing the partnership]

WHAT [YOUR COMPANY] COMMITS TO
- [specific commitment 1]
- [specific commitment 2]
- [specific commitment 3]

WHAT [PARTNER COMPANY] COMMITS TO  
- [specific commitment 1]
- [specific commitment 2]
- [specific commitment 3]

REFERRAL ARRANGEMENT
[Describe referral fee or reciprocal referral arrangement, or state "no financial arrangement"]

CONFIDENTIALITY
Both parties treat client information and business details as confidential.

REVIEW
Both parties review this agreement annually. Either party may terminate
with 30 days written notice.

Signed: _________________ Date: _________  [Your company]
Signed: _________________ Date: _________  [Partner company]
```

## Part 6 – Assign responsibility

The partner tier structure fails if no one owns it.

| Responsibility | Owner | Frequency |
|---|---|---|
| Partner tier reviews | | ○ Quarterly ○ Annually |
| Partner contact cadence (Tier 1) | | Monthly |
| Partner contact cadence (Tier 2) | | Quarterly |
| Tracking partner-originated revenue | | Monthly |
| Onboarding new partners | | As needed |
| Managing partner agreements | | Annual renewal |

## Part 7 – Track and review

Use a simple dashboard. Review quarterly.

**Partner health metrics to track:**

| Metric | Tier 1 target | Tier 2 target |
|---|---|---|
| Leads received from partner (last quarter) | | |
| Leads sent to partner (last quarter) | | |
| Co-sell deals in progress | | |
| Co-sell deals closed (last quarter) | | |
| Joint revenue (last quarter) | | |
| Last executive contact | | |
| MDF activities (last 12 months) | | |

**Tier review questions (quarterly):**
- Have any Tier 2 partners earned promotion to Tier 1?
- Have any Tier 1 partners become inactive enough to move to Tier 2?
- Are there Community Partners ready to become Active Partners?
- Are there relationships that should be ended?

## Common mistakes in partner programme design

| Mistake | What to do instead |
|---|---|
| Creating too many tiers | Start with 3. Add more only when 3 is genuinely insufficient |
| No criteria for tier assignment | Define minimum criteria before assigning anyone |
| Tiers without benefits | Partners must understand what they get at each tier |
| No owner for the programme | Name one person responsible, even part-time |
| Building the programme before the relationships | Programme structure follows active relationships |
| Treating all partner types the same | Technology partners and referral partners need different structures |
