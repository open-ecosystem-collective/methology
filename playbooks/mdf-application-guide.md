# MDF Application Guide

**Open Ecosystem Collective – Market Development Funds: From Zero to First Application**

Most small Cloud Native companies are sitting on USD 10'000–40'000 per year
in unclaimed Market Development Funds. This guide shows you how to claim it.

**For:** Companies with Assessment score 16–30 (MDF Readiness D2 score ≥ 6)  
**Delivered by:** Level 2+  
**License:** CC-BY-SA 4.0

---

## What is MDF?

Market Development Funds are budgets that software vendors allocate to
their partners for joint marketing activities. The vendor pays. You execute.

**What MDF typically funds:**
- Joint webinars and virtual events
- Conference sponsorships and booth costs
- Content creation (case studies, whitepapers, videos)
- Paid advertising featuring the vendor's product alongside your services
- Training and certification costs
- Customer proof-of-concept projects

**What MDF does not fund:**
- Your staff salaries
- General business expenses
- Activities unrelated to the vendor's products

**To find out if MDF is available:**  
Search for `[vendor name] partner MDF` or `[vendor name] co-marketing funds`.
Most major vendors have a partner portal where MDF is listed under
"partner benefits" or "co-marketing".

---

## Step 2 – Find the right contact

MDF is not approved by the sales rep who signs your partner agreement.
It is approved by a different team – usually called Partner Marketing,
Alliance Marketing, or Channel Marketing.

**How to find the contact:**
1. Log into the vendor's partner portal
2. Look for "Partner Marketing", "MDF", or "Co-Marketing" in the menu
3. If not visible, email your partner manager and ask:
   *"Who is the right person to speak to about co-marketing funds?"*

**Save this contact.** You will use it for every future application.

---

## Step 3 – Choose the right activity

Do not apply for MDF for something you plan to do anyway.
Apply for MDF for something you will do because the funds exist.

**Best first activities for small Cloud Native companies:**

**Joint webinar (recommended for first application)**  
- Low cost to produce
- Clear metrics (registrations, attendees)
- Easy to demonstrate ROI to the vendor
- Typical MDF range: USD 1'000–5'000

**Case study production**  
- High value to vendor (they want proof points)
- Reusable asset for you
- Typical MDF range: USD 2'000–8'000

**Conference presence**  
- KubeCon, CloudNativeCon, HashiConf, etc.
- Booth or sponsorship costs eligible
- Typical MDF range: USD 5'000–20'000

## Step 4 – Write the application

Most vendor MDF applications are forms in the partner portal.
They typically ask for:

**Activity description** (2–3 sentences)
> We plan to host a joint webinar with [vendor] targeting DevOps and Platform
> Engineering teams at mid-size enterprises in the DACH region. The webinar
> will cover [topic] and feature a live demo of [vendor product] integrated
> with our [service]. Expected reach: 200 registrations, 80 live attendees.

**Budget breakdown**

| Item | Cost |
|---|---|
| Webinar platform | USD |
| Promotion / paid social | USD |
| Design / production | USD |
| **Total requested** | **USD** |

**Expected outcomes**
> - 200 registrations, 80 live attendees
> - 3–5 qualified leads per company
> - Recording available for 90-day follow-up campaign
> - Joint case study published within 60 days of event

**Timeline**
> Planning: [date] · Promotion: [date] · Event: [date] · Reporting: [date]

---

## Step 5 – Submit and follow up

Submit through the partner portal. Then:

- **Within 24 hours:** Send a brief email to your contact confirming submission
  and offering to answer questions
- **After 7 days:** Follow up if you have not received a response
- **After approval:** Send a calendar invite for the activity and a
  brief project plan showing milestones

Most approvals come within 5–15 business days.
Rejections almost always come with feedback – use it to improve the next application.

---

## Step 6 – Execute and report

After the activity, vendors require a proof-of-execution report. This typically includes:
- Screenshots of the promotion
- Attendance numbers
- Leads generated
- Invoice receipts

**File these immediately after the activity.** Do not wait until the reporting
deadline. Slow or missing reports damage your relationship for future applications.

---

## Common reasons MDF applications are rejected

| Reason | How to avoid it |
|---|---|
| Activity already completed before approval | Always get approval before spending |
| Budget items not clearly itemised | Break down every cost line by line |
| No clear vendor logo/mention in the activity | Include the vendor visibly in all materials |
| Outcomes too vague | Give specific numbers – registrations, leads, reach |
| Wrong partner tier | Check minimum tier requirements before applying |
| Submitted to wrong team | Find the partner marketing contact, not the sales rep |

## Vendor-specific notes

**AWS Partner Network (APN)**  
MDF called "Co-Op Funds". Accessible via APN Partner Central.
Minimum tier: Select. Process: Request → Approval → Execute → Claim.
Typical timeline: 2–3 weeks for approval.

**Google Cloud Partner Advantage**  
MDF called "Partner Marketing Funds". Via Google Partner Advantage portal.
Requires Partner Advantage status. Higher reimbursement rates for specialisations.

**HashiCorp / IBM**  
MDF via HashiCorp Partner Portal. Select and Premier tiers eligible.
Strong preference for activities featuring HashiCorp products prominently.

**Datadog**  
MDF via Datadog Partner Network portal. Partner status required.
Strong focus on technical content – webinars and workshops preferred.

**Grafana Labs**  
MDF via Grafana Partner Portal. Silver tier and above.
Open source community activities often considered positively.
